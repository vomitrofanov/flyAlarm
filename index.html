Features

Built for the decision, not the forecast.

Weather apps show you data. FlyAlarm evaluates it against each site's launch conditions and your personal limits — and gives you an answer.

Site-aware wind analysis

Every site is evaluated against its own takeoff sector. Wind aligned with launch scores well; crosswind or out-of-sector conditions are flagged, regardless of how calm the forecast looks.

Personal risk profiles

Conservative, Standard or Performance — each profile applies its own thresholds for wind speed, gusts, gust differential, thermal strength and instability. The same day reads differently for different pilots.

Defined flying windows

Hour-by-hour scoring identifies when conditions open and close, so a notification means a concrete window — for example 11:00 to 17:00 — not a general impression of the day.

Notifications with restraint

FlyAlarm stays silent below your quality threshold. When it does notify you, it includes the site, the score and the window — everything needed to decide whether to go.

How it works

Three steps, then it runs on its own.

01

Choose your location

Set a home point and a search radius from 25 to 200 km. FlyAlarm identifies every registered flying site within range.

02

Set your profile

Select the risk profile that matches your experience and wing. All scoring thresholds adjust accordingly.

03

Receive your alerts

Conditions are re-evaluated hourly across a 3-day outlook. When a site clears your threshold, you're notified with the details.
